A **finding** is one specific issue chifu spotted — a missing header, an exposed
banner, a dependency with a known CVE. Each scan produces findings; the **Findings**
page aggregates them across all your scans so you have one to-do list.

## The Findings page

Open **Findings** in the sidebar to see everything chifu has flagged for your
domains. The table shows, per row:

- **Severity** — how urgent it is (see below).
- **Finding** — the title, plus its ID, any CVE, and CVSS score.
- **Target** — which domain it's on.
- **Tool** — which check produced it.
- **Status** — where it stands (Open by default).
- **Age** — how long ago it was discovered.

### Filtering

Use the **search box** to filter by title, target, ID, or tool. Toggle the
**Critical / High / Medium / Low** chips to focus on a severity. The count on the right
updates as you filter.

## Severities

Fix from the top down. Severity reflects both how exploitable an issue is and how much
damage it could do.

Some passive checks also produce **Info** findings — observations with no direct risk
(like a missing `security.txt`).

## Statuses

Each finding carries a status so you can track your progress:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Open** | Newly found and not yet handled. |
| **Fixed** | You resolved it. |
| **Dismissed** | Not relevant to you. |
| **Accepted** | A known, accepted risk. |

## Known vulnerabilities (CVEs)

When chifu matches your detected stack to a published CVE, it shows up in the **Known
vulnerabilities** table on the scan report, with the CVE ID, severity, affected version
range, and a link to the official NVD entry. These are usually the highest-impact items
and the fastest wins — bumping a dependency version often clears several at once.

## Working through findings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sort by severity">
    Start with Critical and High. The dashboard already ranks them first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the plain-English explanation">
    Each finding explains what's wrong and why it matters — no jargon required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the fix">
    Paste the suggested fix into your editor or your AI assistant (Cursor, v0, Lovable)
    and ship it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-scan to confirm">
    Run the scan again. Fixed items drop your risk and show up as a green delta on the
    [risk score](/risk-score).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Findings update **live** — if a scan finishes while you're on the page, new findings
  appear without a refresh.
</Note>
