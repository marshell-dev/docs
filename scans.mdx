title: "How scans work"

---

A scan points chifu's engine at your **live** site and runs it through a battery of
checks — the same kinds of probes an attacker would run, minus the damage. You don't
install anything. You don't change your code. You just need a [verified domain](/domains).

## Run a scan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Scans → Run scan now">
    The card on the left of the **Scans** page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a verified domain">
    Only verified domains appear in the dropdown. Need one? See [Domains](/domains).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Scan">
    The button shows **Queuing…**, then your scan appears in **Scan history** below.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Each scan counts against your monthly quota — 3 on Free, 30 on Pro, 120 on Max.
  See [Plans](/plans).
</Note>

## Scan statuses

Your scan moves through these states, and the dashboard updates **live** — no need to
refresh.

| Status | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Queued** | Accepted and waiting for a worker to pick it up. |
| **Running** | Actively probing your site right now. |
| **Completed** | Finished — the full report is ready. |
| **Failed** | Something went wrong (e.g. the site was unreachable). The report shows why. |

<Tip>
  Most static Next.js sites finish in seconds. Larger or slower sites can take a few
  minutes. You can leave the page — the scan keeps running, and history updates when
  it lands.
</Tip>

## Scan history

The **Scan history** table lists your recent scans with their target, ID, start time,
duration, and findings count. The newest three are shown by default; expand to see the
rest. Click any row to open its full report.

## What a report contains

Open a completed scan to see:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Risk score" icon="gauge" href="/risk-score">
    A single 0–99 number with a color band, plus the five-part breakdown of what drove it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tech stack" icon="layer-group">
    What chifu detected powering your site — framework, host, CDN — with versions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Known vulnerabilities" icon="shield-halved">
    A table of CVEs matched to your detected versions, each linking out to NVD.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Findings" icon="bug" href="/findings">
    Everything else we flagged — missing headers, exposed banners, and more.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## On-demand vs. scheduled

Free plans run scans **on demand** — you click the button. On **Pro** and **Max** you
can also [schedule recurring scans](/schedule) so chifu re-checks your site
automatically and catches regressions before your users do.
