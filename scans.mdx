title: "How monitoring works"
description: "Connect a site once; chifu keeps scanning it and surfaces anything new."
---

Monitoring is the heart of chifu. You don't run scans by hand — you connect a
[verified domain](/domains) and chifu re-checks it **automatically on a schedule**,
so a new CVE or a regression after a deploy gets caught within hours, not whenever
you remember to look.

Each check points chifu's engine at your **live** site and runs it through a
battery of probes. Nothing to install, no code changes.

## Automatic vs. on-demand

- **Automatic (the default).** chifu [schedules scans for you](/schedule) at
  load-balanced times and re-runs them on a cadence that matches your plan. This
  is what makes it *monitoring* and not a one-off tool.
- **On-demand.** Want a result right now — say, just after a deploy? **Scans →
  Run scan now**, pick a verified domain, click **Scan**.

<Note>
  Every scan — automatic or manual — counts against your monthly quota (3 on Free,
  30 on Pro, 120 on Max). See [Plans](/plans).
</Note>

## Live status

Scans move through `Queued → Running → Completed` and the dashboard updates live.
Most static sites finish in seconds; larger sites take a few minutes. You can
leave the page — it keeps running.

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Queued** | Accepted, waiting for a worker. |
| **Running** | Actively probing your site. |
| **Completed** | Done — the full report is ready. |
| **Failed** | Something went wrong (e.g. unreachable). The report says why. |

## Hear about it automatically

The point of monitoring is not having to check. Wire up [alerts](/alerts) and
chifu pings you on Discord the moment a scan finds something serious — so the
dashboard is where you go to *fix*, not to *discover*.
