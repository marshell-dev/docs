Monitoring is only useful if it reaches you. Connect an alert channel and chifu
notifies you when a scan completes or turns up something serious — with the
finding and its fix — so the dashboard becomes where you go to *fix*, not to
*discover*.

## Discord webhook

The fastest way to get alerts today.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a webhook in Discord">
    In your server: **Server Settings → Integrations → Webhooks → New Webhook**.
    Pick the channel you want chifu to post in, then **Copy Webhook URL**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste it into chifu">
    Open **Settings → Discord**, paste the URL, and **Save**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test">
    Hit **Send test** to confirm a message lands in your channel. Done — you're
    wired up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The webhook URL is a secret — anyone with it can post to your channel. Don't
  commit it or share it. You can rotate it from Discord any time and paste the new
  one in.
</Note>

## What triggers an alert

- A **scan completes** with new findings.
- Something **critical or high** shows up — including a **newly published CVE**
  that now affects your detected stack.

That last one is the heart of chifu: you hear about a vulnerability *because it
just started affecting you*, not because you happened to run a scan.

## Fix it from the alert

Every alert points at a finding with a plain-English fix. To close the loop
without copy-pasting, connect the [chifu MCP](/ai-editor) and let your AI editor
apply the fix in your code, then re-scan to confirm.
