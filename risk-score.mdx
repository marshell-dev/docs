title: "Your risk score"
description: "One number that tells you how exposed your site is — and the five things that move it."
---

After every scan, chifu boils everything it found into a single **risk score** from
**0 to 99**. The gauge is color-coded so you can read it without thinking.

## Reading the gauge

**Higher is safer.** A high score with a green band means your site is in good shape;
a low score with a red band means there's work to do.

| Band | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Excellent** | Locked down. Nothing serious to act on. |
| **Good** | Solid, with minor things worth tidying up. |
| **Fair** | Some real gaps — worth a look this week. |
| **Poor** | Multiple issues dragging you down. Fix soon. |
| **Critical** | Serious exposure. Treat as urgent. |

Next to the gauge you'll see **confidence dots** (1–3). More dots means chifu had more
signal to work with — for example, a site that returned rich responses gives a more
confident score than one that blocked most probes.

## The five components

The **Score breakdown** splits your score into five areas. Each gets its own 0–100
sub-score and a bar.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Vulnerabilities" icon="shield-halved">
    Known CVEs matched to the framework and dependency versions chifu detected on your
    site. This is usually the biggest mover.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="TLS" icon="certificate">
    Your HTTPS certificate, protocol version, and cipher strength.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Security headers" icon="lock">
    Response headers like `Content-Security-Policy`, `X-Frame-Options`, and HSTS — the
    ones AI scaffolding tends to skip.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Exposure" icon="eye">
    Leaked information: exposed secrets, server banners, and anything public that
    shouldn't be.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Defensive posture" icon="shield">
    How well your site pushes back — CORS policy, error handling, and similar signals.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  **A sub-score of 0 does not mean "zero problems."** Each sub-score is 0–100 where
  **higher means safer**. A **Vulnerabilities: 0** means chifu found serious issues and
  the sub-score bottomed out — check the **Known vulnerabilities** table below it. A
  *high* number is the good one.
</Warning>

## What hurt each score

Click any component row to expand its **hit list** — the specific things that pulled
that score down. That's your to-do list, ordered by where it'll move the needle most.

## Re-scans and deltas

When you scan the same site again, chifu compares it to the previous run. If a
component changed, you'll see a **delta** — a small arrow and number next to the score:

- **Green ↑** — it got better since last time.
- **Red ↓** — it got worse (e.g. a new CVE landed in a dependency).

This makes it easy to spot regressions right after a deploy.

<Tip>
  Scanning after every meaningful deploy turns chifu into a safety net: scheduled scans
  ([see Schedule](/schedule)) do this automatically on Pro and Max.
</Tip>
