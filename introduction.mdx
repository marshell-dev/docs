You vibe-coded a site on Lovable, v0, Bolt, or Cursor and shipped it to Vercel.
It looks great. But did the AI leave a secret in your bundle? Forget a security
header? Ship a dependency with a known CVE?

**chifu** scans your live site like an attacker would, then hands you a plain-English
report and copy-paste fixes. No security degree required.

## What chifu checks

Every scan runs a battery of checks against your live site — no agent to install,
nothing to configure in your code.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Known vulnerabilities" icon="shield-halved">
    Detects your framework and dependencies (Next.js, React, Vercel, Cloudflare…)
    and matches them against known CVEs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Security headers" icon="lock">
    Checks for missing `Content-Security-Policy`, `X-Frame-Options`, HSTS,
    and friends — the headers AI tools love to forget.
  </Card>
  <Card title="TLS & certificates" icon="certificate">
    Verifies your HTTPS certificate, protocol version, and cipher strength.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Exposure & leaks" icon="eye">
    Looks for exposed secrets, server banners, and information leaks that
    shouldn't be public.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who it's for

chifu is built for **vibe-coders** — people who ship real products with AI tools
but never learned application security. If you can read English, you can read a
chifu report. Every finding comes with:

- A **plain-English explanation** of what's wrong and why it matters.
- A **fix** you can paste straight into your editor or AI assistant.
- A **severity** so you know what to fix first (and what can wait).
